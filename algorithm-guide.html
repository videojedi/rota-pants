Xrota Auto-Scheduling Algorithm

A Guide for HR and Management

Overview

The auto-generate feature creates fair, balanced staff schedules that ensure proper coverage while respecting rest requirements and distributing weekend work fairly.

What It Does

For Each Staff Member, Every Week:

  • Assigns exactly 37.5 working hours (5 shifts x 7.5 hours)
  • Gives exactly 2 days off (consecutive when possible)
  • Ensures 12 hours minimum rest between shifts

For The Business:

  • Maintains 1-2 staff coverage at all times during opening hours (08:00-20:30)
  • Allows up to 3 staff during weekday mornings (08:00-16:30) for handovers
  • Limits weekends to maximum 2 staff at any time

Fairness Rules

Weekend Rotation

  • Only 1 person per week gets the "golden weekend" (both Saturday AND Sunday off)
  • The system tracks weekend shifts over a 10-week rolling period
  • Staff who have worked more weekends recently get priority for weekend days off

Shift Variety

  • The system avoids giving anyone the same shift type repeatedly
  • Mix of Early, Mid, Late, and Floating shifts throughout the week

Shift Types

Shift Hours Time Notes
Early 7.5 hrs 08:00 - 16:30 Opens the business
Mid 7.5 hrs 10:00 - 18:30 Covers midday peak
Late 7.5 hrs 12:00 - 20:30 Closes the business
Floating 7.5 hrs 08:30 - 17:00 Fills gaps, flexible coverage

Note: Floating shifts are used to fill gaps and help staff reach their 37.5 hour target. They don't count toward coverage limits.

Rest Requirements

12-Hour Rest Rule:

  • No Late shift followed by Early shift the next day (would be less than 12 hours rest)
  • This also applies across weeks - a Late on Sunday means no Early on Monday the following week

How Days Off Are Assigned

  1. The system tries to give consecutive days off (e.g., Tue-Wed, Thu-Fri)
  2. One person each week gets Saturday-Sunday off (rotates fairly over time)
  3. Maximum 2 staff off on any single day to maintain coverage
  4. If someone can't work weekends due to coverage limits, they get weekday days off plus a Floating shift to make up their hours

Warnings You Might See

Warning What It Means
"Days off are not consecutive" Staff has 2 days off but they're split (e.g., Mon and Sat)
"Only X hrs assigned" Staff couldn't reach 37.5 hours due to constraints
"Coverage gap" A time slot has no staff coverage
"Too many staff" More than 2 staff scheduled at the same time

Tips for Best Results

  • Generate 10 weeks at once for better weekend balancing across the period
  • Review warnings by clicking the summary banner after generation
  • Manually adjust any individual shifts by clicking on cells in the schedule
  • Use Staff Preferences to set preferred days off or shift types for individual staff
